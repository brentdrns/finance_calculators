Use a nominal (not inflation-adjusted) return — e.g. 8% for a broad stock index, which implies ~5% real at 3% inflation. This keeps the math consistent: your mortgage payments are fixed nominal dollars, so the portfolio must be in the same units. Both scenarios are compared in nominal dollars, so the difference between them is still meaningful even though the absolute figures grow with inflation. Every dollar not spent on your mortgage goes into this portfolio — payment savings, interest tax savings, and freed-up cash after payoff. Closing costs and extra paydown are treated as money pulled out on day one.
